# Public EV Charging Landscape in the US

As our company moves towards an all-electric sales fleet, understanding the public EV charging infrastructure in the United States is essential. This briefing outlines the number of public charging points and highlights two of the major charging networks available.

## Public Charging Points

Currently, there are approximately **204,000 public EV charging ports** across the United States, according to 2024 estimates. The charging stations have seen significant growth, more than doubling from previous years, with an increasing number of chargers added weekly. This infrastructure is critical as the adoption of electric vehicles continues to rise, ensuring that users have more options for recharging their vehicles during travel.

## Major Charging Networks

### Tesla Supercharger

The **Tesla Supercharger network** stands as the largest and most prominent charging infrastructure, boasting over **33,400 charging ports** across the country. This network offers a user-friendly experience, with fast charging capabilities and strategic placement along highways, making it ideal for long-distance travel. Tesla's reliability and speed in charging are significant factors for fleets relying on quick turnarounds.

### Electrify America

**Electrify America** is the second major player in the public charging landscape, featuring **nearly 5,100 charging ports**. This network provides a wide range of charging speeds, including DC Fast Charging, which enables quicker recharges for electric vehicles. Electrify America focuses on expanding its locations and services, aiming to cater not just to Tesla vehicles but a broader range of electric vehicles.
